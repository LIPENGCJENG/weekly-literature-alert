## 1. Multihydrogen-bond-bridged composite solid electrolytes enabling continuous Li <sup>+</sup> pathways for stable solid-state lithium batteries

**Authors**: Xin Jia, Xinyu Da, Yanyang Qin, Yuxin Ouyang, Yuanzhang Zhao, N Li et al.
**Journal/Platform**: Science Advances
**SCI Impact Factor / JCR Quartile**: 14.8 / Q1(EasyScholar)
**Publication Date**: 2026-06-24
**DOI**: [https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aed5972](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aed5972)
**Relevance Score**: 5.41/10

### 1. What problem is it really trying to solve?

The study addresses the poor interface compatibility between garnet-type ceramic fillers (LLZTO) and polymer matrices in composite solid electrolytes, specifically caused by the Li2CO3 passivation layer, which leads to particle agglomeration and discontinuous Li+ transport pathways.

### 2. What contribution does it claim?

The authors claim to have developed a chemical conversion strategy to transform the Li2CO3 surface layer into a brushlike polymer (PEGMA-co-UPyMA) that facilitates hydrogen-bond coupling with a dynamic supramolecular ionic conducting polymer, enabling high ceramic loading (90 wt %) while maintaining structural homogeneity.

### 3. What are its main conclusions?

The authors conclude that the resulting composite electrolyte establishes continuous Li+ transport pathways, which they claim leads to improved electrochemical cycling stability in Li|LiFePO4, Li|NMC811, and pouch cell configurations.

## 2. Surface‐Functionalized LLZO‐Incorporated Multilayer Composite Solid Electrolytes for Dendrite Suppression and Efficient Ionic Conduction in Lithium–Metal Batteries

**Authors**: Fazal Ur Rehman, Minhong Woo, Hyesoo Choi, Jihwan Kim, Yong Wook Kim, Sanghee Park et al.
**Journal/Platform**: Advanced Materials
**SCI Impact Factor / JCR Quartile**: 29.6 / Q1(EasyScholar)
**Publication Date**: 2026-06-25
**DOI**: [https://doi.org/10.1002/adma.73879](https://doi.org/10.1002/adma.73879)
**Relevance Score**: 3.81/10

### 1. What problem is it really trying to solve?

The study addresses the simultaneous challenges of lithium dendrite growth, poor interfacial contact, and low ionic conductivity in solid polymer electrolytes for lithium-metal batteries.

### 2. What contribution does it claim?

The authors propose a tri-layered composite solid electrolyte (CSE) architecture using polydopamine-coated LLZO and a block copolymer (PPP) additive to decouple and improve ionic transport and mechanical robustness.

### 3. What are its main conclusions?

The authors conclude that the optimized CSE-30 composite achieves enhanced ionic conductivity (5.60 × 10^-3 S cm^-1 at 60°C) and improved electrochemical stability, with experimental data demonstrating extended cycle life in Li/Li symmetric cells and Li/LFP full cells.

## 3. A Polymer Electrolyte for Rechargeable Magnesium Batteries Synergistically Constructed Based on Deep Eutectic Electrolytes and Polymer Network

**Authors**: Peng Yang, Xiaoping Liang, Yu Wang, Yuan Yuan, Ligang Zhang, Li Gao et al.
**Journal/Platform**: Small
**SCI Impact Factor / JCR Quartile**: 12.1 / Q1(EasyScholar)
**Publication Date**: 2026-06-29
**DOI**: [https://doi.org/10.1002/smll.74365](https://doi.org/10.1002/smll.74365)
**Relevance Score**: 2.94/10

### 1. What problem is it really trying to solve?

The insufficient understanding of the synergistic interactions between deep eutectic electrolytes (DEEs) and polymer networks, which limits the optimization of ionic conductivity, electrochemical stability, and interfacial compatibility in rechargeable magnesium batteries.

### 2. What contribution does it claim?

The authors claim to have developed a deep eutectic polymer electrolyte (DEPE) using MgCl2, 2-chloro-N,N-dimethylacetamide (CDMA), and poly(ethylene glycol) diacrylate (PEGDA), asserting that the polymer network modulates the solvation structure to enhance Mg2+-carbonyl coordination and suppress Cl- migration.

### 3. What are its main conclusions?

The study concludes that the incorporation of the polymer network improves the magnesium ion transference number (0.665), ionic conductivity (3.85 × 10^-4 S cm^-1), and electrochemical stability window (2.469 V) compared to the neat DEE, resulting in improved cycling stability in Mg||Mo6S8 cells.

## 4. Water-Fed, Photovoltaic-Driven Anion-Exchange Membrane Water Electrolysis for Solar Hydrogen Production

**Authors**: Melissa E. Kreider, William A. Callahan, Todd G. Deutsch, James L. Young
**Journal/Platform**: ACS Applied Energy Materials
**SCI Impact Factor / JCR Quartile**: 5.5 / Q2(EasyScholar)
**Publication Date**: 2026-06-28
**DOI**: [https://doi.org/10.1021/acsaem.6c01262](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsaem.6c01262)
**Relevance Score**: 0.27/10

### 1. What problem is it really trying to solve?

The lack of a demonstrated water-fed, photovoltaic-driven anion-exchange membrane (AEM) water electrolysis system capable of operating under low-temperature, diurnal-cycling conditions using PGM-free catalysts.

### 2. What contribution does it claim?

The authors provide a proof-of-concept demonstration of a direct-coupled PV-AEM electrolyzer system using a commercial Si mini module, passive heating, and passive water flow, evaluated through on-sun testing.

### 3. What are its main conclusions?

The system achieved an average solar-to-hydrogen (STH) efficiency of 6.6% over 11 days of diurnal cycling with no observed efficiency loss or significant corrosion, suggesting the viability of using inexpensive materials for distributed hydrogen production.

The study addresses the challenge of accurately quantifying the electronic conductivity of lithium thiophosphate-based solid electrolytes, which is often obscured by ionic contributions and experimental artifacts in standard measurement techniques.
